# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome! This README provides an overview and links to all OctoAcme project management process guides found in the `docs/` folder. Whether you are onboarding as a new team member or returning as an experienced contributor looking for a consistent reference, this document is your starting point for understanding how OctoAcme runs projects.

---

## Overview

OctoAcme uses an iterative, customer-centric, and data-informed project management process. Key steps include:

- **Initiation:** Define the business need, clarify stakeholders, align on goals, and prepare a one-pager summary before planning.
- **Planning:** Break initiatives into actionable backlogs, estimate scope, address risks/dependencies, and document acceptance criteria.
- **Execution & Tracking:** Drive progress via regular standups and syncs, maintain transparency with project boards, and follow quality/testing standards.
- **Risks & Communication:** Maintain a risk register, escalate blockers, and keep stakeholders informed using standardized templates.
- **Release & Deployment:** Follow a checklist-driven approach for safe, repeatable releases with rollback and communication protocols.
- **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement:** After milestones and incidents, capture learnings, track improvement actions, and foster a learning environment.
- **Roles & Responsibilities:** Clear definitions for Product Managers, Project Managers, Developers, QAs, and Stakeholders ensure ownership and smooth execution.

## Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme's project management processes are structured to foster collaboration, transparency, and continuous improvement across cross-functional teams. The approach begins with clear project initiation, where a business need is validated, stakeholders are identified, and success metrics are defined. A Project One-pager aligns everyone on goals, scope, and timeline. Once approved, planning proceeds with a prioritized backlog, estimation of effort, identification of dependencies and risks, and a release timeline. The team leverages templates and checklists to ensure each initiative starts with a solid foundation and a shared understanding of objectives and outcomes.

Defined roles form the backbone of OctoAcme's methodology. Core personas include Project Managers (PMs) who coordinate schedules, risks, and communications; Product Managers (PdMs) who own the product vision and backlog; Developers who implement and test features; and QA specialists who uphold release quality. Each role has specific responsibilities, often collaborating through planning meetings, code reviews, and retrospectives. Stakeholders provide essential feedback and approvals, ensuring alignment with broader business objectives.

Workflow execution is anchored by regular team rhythms: daily standups to address progress and blockers, weekly delivery syncs for broader updates and risk sharing, and milestone demos for stakeholder engagement. Work items are visualized on a project board moving through states like Backlog, In Progress, QA, and Done, with pull request (PR) guidelines emphasizing smaller, testable increments and robust review practices. Blockers are escalated systematically, from team-level triage to PM and sponsor involvement based on severity and impact.

Quality and communication are integral throughout the process. Quality assurance is embedded via automated and manual testing—unit, integration, and end-to-end smoke tests, with CI pipelines running linting and security scans. Risks and dependencies are meticulously cataloged in a Risk Register, reviewed weekly, and communicated with stakeholders through standardized templates. Regular retrospectives drive continuous improvement by capturing learnings and translating them into actionable backlog items, reinforcing a culture where measuring impact and iterating based on evidence are paramount.
